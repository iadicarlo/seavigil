# Measuring SeaVigil: how good are the leads?

> Generated by `scripts/build_measurement.py`. Re-run to refresh.

SeaVigil produces inspection **leads, not verdicts**. This reports what can be measured from public data, and is honest about what cannot.

## Recall: does it catch known IUU vessels?

Ground truth: the [CCAMLR Non-Contracting-Party IUU list](https://www.ccamlr.org/en/compliance/non-contracting-party-iuu-vessel-list), an independent authority (checked 2026-06-30).
- **8 of 18** listed vessels resolve in the GFW registry SeaVigil queries; the other **10** predate or evade AIS, the dark fleet the SAR layer targets, not an AIS-layer miss.
- **8 of 8** resolved vessels have no current fishing authorization, so SeaVigil grades them unauthorized: **recall on the AIS-visible IUU fleet is 8/8**.
- **5** are independently tagged `IUU` in GFW's registry, corroborating CCAMLR.

## Precision of the IUU cross-reference

- Strong-identifier (IMO) matching: **18/18** recall on the listed fleet, **0** false positives on a 10-vessel legitimate control.
- Name matching reaches **81/82** aliases, but **42 of 82** aliases are short generic words that could collide with unrelated vessels, so a name-only match is surfaced as "verify", not asserted.

## The deployed system discriminates (it does not flag everything)

Authorization grading across **628** real monitored incidents:

| status | count | share |
|---|---|---|
| no_record | 274 | 44% |
| domestic | 133 | 21% |
| authorized | 119 | 19% |
| unverifiable | 74 | 12% |
| authorization_lapsed | 14 | 2% |
| flag_unknown | 14 | 2% |

**40%** are cleared (authorized, or in their own EEZ) and **46%** are foreign with no authorization on record and flagged for review. The layer separates the two; it is not a blanket flag.

## The honest bound

The **true false-positive rate of the authorization layer cannot be measured from public data**: national EEZ fishing licences are not public, so "no authorization on record" can mean *unlicensed* or *licensed under a record we cannot see*. That is exactly why SeaVigil outputs prioritized leads, not proof, and why a human verifies before any enforcement. Full event-level precision and recall would need a labelled set of confirmed IUU events with positions and times, plus a matched legitimate control with their licences known; that dataset is not public.
